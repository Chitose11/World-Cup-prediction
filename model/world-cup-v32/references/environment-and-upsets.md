Heat tactical meaning: high-press teams such as Germany, Netherlands, Uruguay/Bielsa-style sides, and some England shapes may see effective pressing time shrink from 90 minutes to 60-70 minutes. Late match windows become counterattack windows for opponents.

Heat adaptation usually needs 7-14 days of repeated exposure. European players often have only 7-10 practical days after club-season rest and travel. Northern European squads are at a larger adaptation disadvantage than Iberian/Mediterranean squads.

Mandatory hydration pauses can protect players but also reset momentum. A team under pressure can regain shape during the pause; a team building pressure can lose rhythm.

## Altitude

Mexico City: 2,240m; Guadalajara: 1,566m; Monterrey: 538m; other venues mostly 0-300m.

Physiology:

- At 2,240m, pressure is about 78% of sea level and oxygen partial pressure about 80%.
- Above 1,200m, total running distance falls about 3.1%.
- Above 1,200m, high-speed running can fall about 21%.
- Peak sprint speed may remain, but repeat sprint ability and recovery decline.
- At 2,240m, VO2max drop is roughly 10%-14% by extrapolation.
- Mexico has the largest altitude-adaptation edge; European sea-level teams arriving 3-5 days early only partially adapt.

Tactical implication: in Mexico City, the last 20-30 minutes are an altitude stress window. Non-adapted teams may show slower decisions and reduced recovery.

Altitude + heat + afternoon kickoff can create a 15%-20% effective performance hit, larger than either variable alone.

## Travel

Travel fatigue is nonlinear:

- Flight under 4 hours: next-day performance drop about 2%-3%.
- Flight over 6 hours: performance drop about 5%-8%.
- Crossing 3+ time zones adds about 3%-5%.

The PDF highlights Mexico as lowest-travel/high-familiarity profile, with a cited group-winner travel estimate around 5,163 km. A Mexico vs high-travel opponent differential can be worth 8-12 percentage points in effective match state.

When exact route is unknown, classify:

| Travel situation | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| same cluster / under 4h | small fatigue |
| 4-8h travel | moderate fatigue |
| over 8h or cross-continent | high fatigue |
| 3+ time zones or eastward travel | add fatigue |
| repeated travel plus short rest | lower late-game intensity |

## Tactical Upset Types

Type I upset: favorite has model win probability above 65%, but loses or fails to win against an opponent whose model probability is at most 35%.

Type II upset: market implied probability and Elo/model probability differ by more than 20 percentage points. This is a pricing divergence, not necessarily a result upset.

Type III upset: non-strength event such as red card, extreme weather, VAR, goalkeeper overperformance, or travel fatigue drives result. Low repeatability.
